# Business Model Categories

## Overview

The Autonomous Business Launcher supports seven distinct business model categories, each with different autonomy potential, revenue characteristics, and operational requirements. Choose based on your skills, risk tolerance, and desired level of involvement.

## The Seven Categories

### 1. Digital Products (Autonomy: 9/10)
Best for: Creators who can produce high-value templates, courses, or AI-generated assets. Revenue comes from one-time sales and subscriptions. Highest autonomy because fulfillment is instant and automated. Start here if you want maximum passive income potential.

### 2. AI-as-a-Service (Autonomy: 8/10)
Best for: Technical builders who can package AI capabilities as managed services. Usage-based and subscription revenue. High ceiling but requires ongoing model maintenance and customer support infrastructure.

### 3. Content Automation (Autonomy: 8/10)
Best for: Marketers and content strategists. Revenue from advertising, affiliate commissions, and sponsorships. AI handles content creation and publishing. Scales with audience size but requires patience for organic growth.

### 4. SaaS / Micro-SaaS (Autonomy: 8/10)
Best for: Developers targeting niche problems. Subscription revenue (MRR) provides predictable income. Micro-SaaS can reach profitability with fewer than 100 paying customers in the right niche.

### 5. Marketplace / Platform (Autonomy: 7/10)
Best for: Network builders who can attract both sides of a market. Commission and listing fee revenue. Hardest to start (chicken-and-egg problem) but most defensible once network effects kick in.

### 6. E-Commerce (Autonomy: 6/10)
Best for: Design-oriented entrepreneurs. Print-on-demand and dropshipping eliminate inventory risk. Lower autonomy due to physical fulfillment dependencies, but AI handles product design and listing optimization.

### 7. AI Trading & Finance (Autonomy: 6/10)
Best for: Quantitative thinkers with finance domain knowledge. Performance fees and subscription revenue. Highest risk category — requires careful backtesting, risk management, and regulatory awareness.

## Selection Criteria

When choosing a category, consider: your technical skills, available capital, time horizon, risk tolerance, and whether you prefer building products or audiences. Most successful autonomous businesses combine two or more categories for diversified revenue.
